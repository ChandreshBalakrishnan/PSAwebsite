This ensures that the prototype meets the requirement, even if the outdoor environment is not particularly bright at the time of the demonstration, due to weather.

O-4.2-STRU-1 The breadboard shall be securely contained such that the breadboard doesn't shift around when shaken or rotated. Test With the prototype fully assembled, record the position of the Red LED relative to its enclosure (height, width, length from walls). Subject the device to manual shaking and rotation along all available directions: left–right translation, forward–backward translation, top–bottom translation, and rotation about each of the three principal axes. After completing the motion sequence, remeasure the same positions. Repeat the full test sequence three times. Verify that the measured positional differences before and after each trial are consistent with zero within a tolerance of ±0.5 mm. This ensures the breadboard has not shifted or rotated. Metric: PASS/FAIL. Video O-4.2-STRU-1.mov shows the prescribed shaking and rotation motions applied to the fully assembled prototype for each trial. Measurement results recorded in O-4.2-STRU-1.pdf show the position of the red LED relative to the enclosure before and after each trial. A ruler was used to obtain these measurements, which is sufficient for verification given the ±0.5 mm tolerance specified. Across all trials, measured positional differences were consistent with zero within tolerance, indicating no detectable translation or rotation of the breadboard. The requirement is satisfied. PASS. The tolerance was based on a conservative estimate of the minimum distance between objects that can be resolved by the average human eye. The derivation can be found in Minimum_Resolution_of_Eye.pdf

O-3.1-SOFT-1 The LED state shall not change unless a user presses a button. Test Operate the fully assembled prototype for a continuous period of 60 seconds without pressing any control buttons while observing the LED state. Repeat this test three times. During each trial, apply mild mechanical disturbances (e.g., lifting or gently shaking the device) and verify that the LED state remains unchanged throughout each observation interval. Metric: PASS/FAIL. Three test trials are documented in videos O-3.1-SOFT-1-1.MOV, O-3.1-SOFT-1-2.MOV, and O-3.1-SOFT-1-3.MOV. In each video, the fully assembled prototype is shown connected to a laptop, with the power cable, microcontroller, and executing code visible throughout the recording, confirming continuous operation. At the start and end of each trial, the LED states are intentionally cycled using button presses to verify that the system is powered and responsive. During each 60-second observation interval, no control buttons are pressed; at approximately the 20-second mark of each trial, the prototype is subjected to mild mechanical disturbances as specified. In all trials, the LED state remains unchanged in the absence of button input. The requirement is satisfied. PASS. Mechanical disturbances were included during testing to verify robustness against unintended state changes due to accidental button presses induced by the structural subsystem.
O-3.1-SOFT-3 There shall be no states achieved by the LEDs other than the 'OFF', 'PLANTS ONLY', and 'PLANTS&PEOPLE' states described in O-1.1-SOFT-1, O-1.2-SOFT-1, and O-2.1-SOFT-1 regardless of user input. Test Execute a structured set of button interaction sequences on the fully assembled prototype while observing the LED outputs. From each operating state (OFF, PLANTS ONLY, PLANTS & PEOPLE), perform the following five input actions: • Single press of the Forward button • Single press of the Backward button • Press-and-hold of the Forward button for 2 s • Press-and-hold of the Backward button for 2 s • Press-and-hold of both buttons simultaneously for 2 s This results in a total of 15 test interactions (5 inputs × 3 states). Repeat procedure three times. Verify that, for all tested interactions, the LEDs only assume the OFF, PLANTS ONLY, and PLANTS & PEOPLE states, and that no other LED combinations occur at any time. Metric: PASS/FAIL This test is documented in videos O-3.1-SOFT-3-1.mov, O-3.1-SOFT-3-2.mov, and O-3.1-SOFT-3-3.mov. In each video, the predefined set of five button interactions is executed from each operating state (OFF, PLANTS ONLY, and PLANTS & PEOPLE), resulting in 15 interactions per trial. The full test sequence is repeated three times. Across all trials, the LED outputs are observed continuously. For every tested interaction, the LEDs only assume the defined OFF, PLANTS ONLY, and PLANTS & PEOPLE states. No unintended LED combinations or undefined states are observed at any point during testing. The requirement is satisfied. PASS. N/A
